# The Hobbit Campaign — Campaign Context Document

## Overview

A hobbit-only campaign run using Old-School Essentials (OSE) / D&D Basic/Expert rules. Three players, playing weekly-ish online sessions via Foundry VTT with an emphasis on physical dice, pencil-and-paper, and minimal digital tools. The campaign began in November 2025 and is ongoing.

The setting is loosely based on the Five Shires region of the Known World/Mystara. There is no confirmed connection to the Forgotten Realms setting used in the same group's separate Daggerdale/Chrysalis campaign (DM'd by Les). The tone is deliberately lighter than their previous Call of Cthulhu game — this campaign was born out of a collective need for escapism during a difficult period for all players.

The campaign draws on classic TSR modules adapted for hobbit play.

## The Players and Their Characters

### Turnip "Stockwell" Bramblebrook — played by Stephen

**Class:** Adventurer (Fighter/Mage/Thief generalist), Level 1 **Title:** Goodhobbit **Alignment:** Chaotic Good (Todd proposes Lawful; debatable)

**Ability Scores:** STR 12, INT 14, WIS 9, DEX 8, CON 11, CHA 9 **HP:** 10 | **AC:** 11 | **Melee:** +1 | **Missile:** 0 | **Init:** -1

**Saving Throws:** D 8, W 9, P 10, B 13, S 12

**Thief Skills (d6 system, roll under to succeed, 6 always fails):** Climb 2, Find/Remove Traps 1, Hear Noise 3, Hide 3 (5 in wilderness if motionless), Move Silently 2, Open Locks 1, Pick Pockets 1

**Spells Known:**

- Protection from Evil (1st level)
- Remove Fear (1st level, bonus from INT 14)

**Cantrip (at-will):** Can untangle any string, rope, net, etc. with a single pull.

**Magic Item from Farmer Fallow's Stall:** Mysterious stockings, later tailored into legwarmers by Orlane's paranoid tailor. Grants extra stamina in exploration mode.

**Equipment of note:** Blowgun with sleep darts, rope, shortbow, various prepper supplies. Carries a notebook/diary in his vest.

**Concept:** A prepper among hobbits. Chose his own nickname "Stockwell" but everyone still calls him Turnip. Perennially carries almost-but-not-quite-the-right-thing-for-the-job. High intelligence, low wisdom — asks the best investigative questions at the worst social moments. Constitutionally incapable of reading a room but genuinely good-hearted. His watchword is "Always be prepared."

**Winter activity:** Became hypervigilant after the Hermit encounter. Catalogued oddities around Thistledown in a notebook (quieter foxes, owls flying wider circles, a baby that never cries, a scarecrow that changed hats). Salted his threshold weekly. Checked windows three times before sleep. Kept the lantern lit all night. Wrote a paranoid but beautifully hobbity diary. Composed the ballad "The Pies of Gomwick Fair" for a storytelling competition he missed.

## The DM — Gus

Runs a tight ship disguised as a loose one. Builds layered situations where every NPC has their own logic and agenda, drops real clues alongside red herrings, and lets the players tie themselves in knots. Committed to improving his DMing through these scenarios before eventually running Call of Cthulhu. Uses ascending AC, item-slot encumbrance (9 equipped / 16 packed), time tracking, and carousing rules. Draws heavily from classic TSR modules adapted for hobbit play. The setting is loosely Mystara's Five Shires.

---

## Campaign Rules

- **System:** OSE / D&D Basic/Expert
- **Character Class:** "Adventurer" — a Fighter/Mage/Thief generalist with a single XP table
- **Magic:** Sorcerer-style (intrinsic spells, no memorization, no spellbook required). Can learn additional spells from scrolls/spellbooks found in play.
- **Thief Skills:** d6 Expertise system (roll under skill score, 6 always fails)
- **AC:** Ascending
- **Encumbrance:** Item-slot based (9 equipped, 16 packed). Packed items take a round to retrieve.
- **Pony (Opal):** Also has 9 equipped / 16 packed slots
- **Carousing:** Spend gold during downtime for bonus XP (with risk of trouble)
- **HP at Level 1:** Roll d6 + keep starting 6 HP as base (bonus for small party survivability)

## Campaign Timeline

### Prologue: Thistledown to Gomwick (Autumn)

The three hobbits set out from the hamlet of Thistledown with Binny, their uncle's pony, loaded with goods to sell at the Gomwick Harvest Festival. They took the Old Road through the forest instead of the North Road due to a delayed departure from a rainstorm.

**Key events on the road:**

- Purchased magical items from Farmer Fallow's roadside stall (whistle, feathers, stockings)
- Experienced a strange mist in the woods and a feeling of being "Seen"
- Encountered Tobias Chubb, a ne'er-do-well who had been talking about trees "looking at him funny"

### Chapter 0: The Gomwick Harvest Festival

**Key events:**

- Boffo won the pie-eating contest, eventually becoming Quarter-Finalist and beyond. Met Gammy Boffin (organizer) and his daughter Daisy (the baker). Also attracted the ire of Grunela Bunce, the defeated favorite.
- Wedge scored two bullseyes in axe-throwing with a new axe from Lotho Longbuck.
- Turnip entered the storytelling competition but missed his quarter-final slot.
- The party foiled an attempted poisoning of the pie competition by Adelard Potts and Hanna Boggs, using Tobias Chubb as their agent. Turnip composed a ballad about it.
- **The Hungry Hank Incident:** A scarecrow ("Hungry Hank") came alive and rampaged through the fair. The official narrative quickly reshaped into "it was just a fire accident." Boffo became the public hero; Wedge and Turnip got disapproving looks.
- Met **Buford Niss** (The Hon. Horace Buford Hockwallop Niss, Esq.), a wealthy, well-traveled old hobbit who lives at Toppo Hill House overlooking Gomwick. He invited them to his estate for private conversation.

### The Hermit Mission

Buford sent the party into the Old Woods to visit "The Hermit" — an ancient tall-folk man living in a cabin. The party experienced deeply unsettling and contradictory things: each saw the Hermit's house differently (Turnip saw a too-tall hobbit-hole, Wedge saw an enormous skull, Boffo saw a drafty old cabin). They found the body of Tobias Chubb inside. They interacted with the Hermit's "strange arcane machine" and each received a prophetic vision:

- **Boffo:** A terrible plague decimating the Shires
- **Turnip:** An immense tidal wave washing over the Shires
- **Wedge:** His flesh rotting away as he walks in undeath

Buford dismissed their concerns about the Hermit being dangerous (or dead/skeletal), paid them, and told them to put it out of their minds.

Each received a strange bronze coin from the Hermit that guided them out of the woods.

### Winter (Level-Up)

The party returned home to Thistledown. Over the winter they leveled from 0th to 1st level, each developing magical abilities and their own winter stories. The prophetic visions returned occasionally in dreams.

In early spring, each received a letter from Buford summoning them back to Gomwick, claiming illness (restricted to "a scant three meals of porridge and fish a day") and needing "young legs" to pursue a matter on his behalf. Enclosed: 1 gold coin + 20 silver for travel expenses.

### Chapter 1: The Road to Orlane

The party traveled to Gomwick, met with the ailing Buford at Toppo Hill House, and received their mission: travel to the village of Orlane to investigate on his behalf. Details of the briefing are sparse in available records.

**On the road to Orlane:**

- Encountered dire wolves (reported to Constable Grover upon arrival in Orlane)
- Acquired Opal, a pony (replacing Binny), stabled at Killian Gade's in Orlane

### Chapter 1.5: Huddle Farm (Side Quest)

En route to Orlane, the party stopped at Huddle Farm near the Sunnyside crossroads. Tolman "Tom" Huddle hired them for 30gp to catch his neighbor Norrie Sutton in the act of sabotage.

**The Sutton-Huddle Dispute:** A feud over access to blackberry bushes. Norrie built a hedge on his property blocking Tom's traditional right-of-way through Deadman's Gap. Escalating sabotage followed.

**Sabotage inventory:**

- Vegetable garden destroyed (torn wool clothing found in dirt)
- Nailer, the family dog, went missing (later found dead in a well, poisoned by tainted toads and consumed by Green Slime)
- Tom's prized wine stolen (dwarf-sized footprints — likely Krund the freeloading dwarf)
- Barn burned down (accidentally — Berry Huddle and Sam Sutton were secretly meeting romantically in the barn, left Sam's dad's pipe unattended)
- Cows painted green (green paint and hobbit footprints found at the ruined tower)

**Key NPCs at Huddle Farm:**

- **Tom Huddle:** Paranoid, sleep-deprived patriarch. Excavating his hill home, rebuilding his barn, feuding with Suttons, and losing his mind.
- **Primula Huddle:** The matriarch, obsessed with cleanliness, near breaking point.
- **Berry Huddle (18):** Secret romance with Sam Sutton. Confessed about the barn fire.
- **Sherry Huddle (16):** Bookish middle child. Claims the house is haunted. Dismissed by siblings.
- **Bordo Huddle (8):** Claims he saw a giant frog-headed demon at the ruined tower. Largely forgotten by the family.
- **Krund Pothrower:** Freeloading dwarf, business partner of Tom's cousin Boris. Sent to rebuild the barn but berated his crew.
- **Epsten:** A juvenile stone giant — the "workforce" rebuilding the barn. On strike until Krund apologizes.

**The Fortfield Tower:** An ancient wizard's tower in the ruins Tom plans to bury. The party explored 5 basement levels:

- B1: Bedroom/quarters — green paint found, painted warning about "the Eye"
- B2: Workshop/laboratory — killed a Crab Spider
- B3: Library — fought a floating crystal security system (AC 4, 12 HP)
- B4: Summoning Room — "The Eye" (beholder-family floating eye trap/puzzle, elemental attacks)
- B5: Unfinished room with natural tunnel connecting to caverns under the well

Tom's wealth and his plans to seal the tower are noted but unexplained.

**Unresolved questions (per Todd):** Why wouldn't Tom have found his own dog in his own vault? Why would Tom smash his own chest? What about the Suttons actually sneaking around? Why hire the party if he did it all? What's going on with the poisoned toads?

### Chapter 2: Orlane — Current Arc

The party arrived in Orlane, a hobbit village on a flat agricultural plain (modeled on the landscape between the Ottawa and St. Lawrence rivers in Eastern Ontario). This is an adaptation of the classic module N1: Against the Cult of the Reptile God (or Gus's version of it).

**The situation in Orlane:** Something is deeply wrong. People are disappearing — some leave and don't return, others vanish for days and come back "changed" (personality altered, sometimes violent). Everyone has a cover story. A new temple to the goddess Merikka has been built on a hill behind 3-meter walls. The priests have become increasingly isolated.

**Key NPCs in Orlane:**

- **Constable Grover:** Local constable, armored in mail with a crossbow. Two non-local deputies (Donovan and Bulbar). The party initially suspected him of being compromised, but Zacharias trusts him, and his reactions to the troglodyte news (blanching, genuine concern) suggest he may be honest. He's been patient with the party despite their repeated troublemaking.

- **Belba:** Proprietress of the Slumbering Serpent Inn. Older, knew Grover from childhood, never liked him. Became a confidante — the party told her everything. She was genuinely shaken by the evidence of violence at the Foaming Mug.

- **Olwyn:** Belba's husband. Serves as messenger.

- **Zacharias:** Retired adventurer, well-traveled, late middle age. Lives across the river. Was fishing when the party arrived. Sent the original letter to Buford requesting help. Extremely paranoid — tore up his own letter, spoke only outdoors by the lakeshore in whispers. Trusts Grover. Convinced the temple priests are the source of the problems.

- **Traver Stokehart:** Zacharias's servant. Quiet, efficient.

- **Zacharias's intelligence:** Four individuals he believes are compromised: the storekeeper **Wilbur Oldbuck**, the blacksmith **Maddie Snowvale** and family, and the carpenter **Quinn Finla** (unmet). Plus the temple priests. Problems started roughly a year to 18 months ago, correlating with the priests' arrival.

- **Derek Desleigh:** A hobbit with a disfiguring scar. Previously seen at the Golden Grain Inn. Encountered inside the temple working with goblins. Wanted the party taken alive. Fled during the temple fight and is still at large somewhere in the temple complex.

- **Misha:** The priestess of the Temple of Merikka. Not hobbit-proportioned — appears human or aquatic/mermaid-like. Healed Boffo with divine magic (a novel experience for the hobbits). Her quarters were found in the temple (bedroom with desk, bed, chest). A body found hidden in a crawlspace beneath the temple may be the real priestess, suggesting Misha is an imposter who murdered and replaced her.

- **Killian Gade:** Looking after Opal the pony at his stable. Friendly.

- **Vilma:** Widowed, lives by the river. Pleasant afternoon with the party.

- **Rolo (at house #19):** Son of Atticus. Came out with a crossbow, fired at the party's feet, told them to keep moving. Then went and knocked at the temple door.

**Key locations:**

- **The Golden Grain Inn:** The third inn in Orlane. Where Derek Desleigh was seen. The party has not stayed here.
- **The Foaming Mug Inn (ruined):** Supposedly abandoned due to economic downturn. Actually shows signs of violent struggle. Troglodytes nesting in the basement (3 pallets, 2 troglodytes present — party killed both).
- **Matty Snowvale's Smithy:** The blacksmith attacked unprovoked, nearly killed Wedge. Turnip put him down with a sleep dart. His wife filed a complaint with Grover.
- **The Temple of Merikka:** On a hill behind 3m walls. The goddess is of farming, fertility, hearth, knowledge, and light (similar to Chauntea + Amaunator). Two priests from the coast, plus followers/acolytes. Used to come into village, now rarely do. The party was rebuffed when they tried to enter.
- **The Elm Grove:** Old stone cottage, half-dug into the ground, clearly inhabited but unoccupied. A weasel seen. Wedge summoned a raven with his whistle and befriended it with food.
- **Zacharias's House:** Stone gargoyle security system (non-differentiating between friend and foe). The party broke in, lit fires, and started cooking before Zacharias returned.

**Zacharias's equipment (original provision — largely expended):**

- Cloak of Elvenkind (loaned, left with Zacharias — hide 5-in-6)
- ~~Potion of Healing (2 doses, 1d6+1 each)~~ — used during temple dungeon
- ~~Potion of Speed (doubles movement for d6 rounds)~~ — status unclear
- Scroll of Sleep — taken by Turnip (status unclear)
- Scroll of Wizard Lock — held by Wedge
- Scroll of Dispel Magic — held by Wedge
- ~~6 special sling bullets: 4x +1, 1x Sleep, 1x Phantasmal Force~~ — all expended or lost
- 100 gold coins (used to pay for healing at temple)

**Items acquired in temple:**

- Chainmail armor, shield, mace (Boffo — equipped)
- Brooch of Shielding (Boffo — absorbs 30 HP damage before breaking)
- 420 gold pieces (140 each)
- Golden carrot (Turnip)
- Manacles and keys (from dead goblins)

**Secret item:** Wedge has a Scroll of ESP — not yet used.

### Session: Entering the Temple and the Dungeon Beneath

**Entering the temple (unrecorded, end of previous session):**

The party entered the temple posing as pilgrims seeking healing, bringing Zacharias's bag of gold. They were received by Misha, the priestess, who healed Boffo with divine magic — a novelty for the hobbits. During the visit they spotted Derek Desleigh, a scarred hobbit previously seen at the Golden Grain Inn, who appeared to be involved with goblins inside the temple. They observed the main worship hall with its large jade statue of Merikka (depicted with cornucopia of fish, shell motifs, flickering lights on pillars).

**The Temple Fight:**

Derek and a group of goblins confronted the party. Derek wanted them taken alive. The party fought back in the darkened worship hall:

- Wedge used a +1 sling bullet to take down Goblin 1. Turnip fired sleep darts at Derek — hit, but Derek saved vs. poison. Turnip bluffed that the poison would take effect in two minutes.
- Boffo was knocked unconscious during the fight.
- The goblins failed a morale check; the surviving goblin and Derek fled north through a door.
- Turnip attempted to tackle Derek as he fled but failed a strength check.
- The party regrouped rather than pursuing into darkness with a man down.

**Exploring the Temple:**

After reviving Boffo and lighting torches:

- Looted dead goblins: manacles and keys, no valuables.
- Found an offering room with golden food items (carrot, beans, wheat — real food apparently transmuted to gold). Turnip took the golden carrot.
- Found Misha's quarters (south): bedroom with desk, bed, chest. Boffo equipped **chainmail armor, a shield, and a mace** found in the room.
- Discovered a **secret passage** under the bed — opened by a torch mechanism. Propped open with the chest.

**The Dungeon Beneath the Temple:**

The party descended through the trapdoor into underground passages:

- Stonework below is older than the temple — confirms the new temple was built over ancient ruins. The hill that "couldn't be dug into" clearly had been.
- Mix of constructed corridors (some with spiked walls), doors, and natural cave passages. Maze-like layout with new internal walls built within larger original spaces.

**Underground Encounters:**

1. **Giant lizard** — Emaciated, dog-sized. Dropped from above a doorway onto the party. Tough fight (AC 6) but killed. Nest found nearby. Spikes on corridor walls may have been to prevent it from climbing.

2. **Buried body** — Behind a secret door, a crawlspace containing a hobbit-length shallow grave. Partially decomposed body in priestly garments. The theory: this was the real priestess of Merikka, murdered and replaced. "The temple was temple-napped."

3. **Underground stream** — Natural caverns with running water and muddy floors. Bare hobbit footprints and booted prints in the mud. Likely connects to the river system — explains how troglodytes move in and out of town.

4. **A document was found** referencing Orlane as a current target and **Gomwick as the next target** — confirming an organized operation expanding outward. The goblins encountered at a bridge on the road to Orlane are likely connected.

5. **Shrieker mushroom** — Fungal alarm creature. The party attacked it but it likely alerted nearby enemies.

6. **Troglodyte waves** — Multiple groups attacked in the underground stream/cave area. The party bottlenecked them in narrow passages. During this fight:
   - The **Sleep sling bullet** missed and was lost in the water.
   - The **Phantasmal Force sling bullet** also missed and was lost in the water. Neither special bullet activated.
   - **Boffo drank a Potion of Growth** (from a previous adventure) and became giant-sized, dramatically increasing his combat effectiveness. Duration extends into next session.
   - Multiple troglodytes killed including a sub-leader. A second shrieker summoned reinforcements.

7. **Troglodyte lair** — Found a locked chest (key kicked up from dirt by Boffo). Contents:
   - **420 gold pieces** (140 each)
   - **Brooch of Shielding** — absorbs up to 30 points of damage before breaking. Given to Boffo.

**Current Party State (end of session):**

The party is still underground in the troglodyte lair. No XP awarded yet (not in a safe location). More to explore on this dungeon level.

**Boffo** is giant-sized (Potion of Growth, duration extends to next session), wearing chainmail + shield, armed with mace, wearing the Brooch of Shielding. Effectively the party's tank.

**Resources remaining:** Torches running low (one burnt out, lantern now in use). Most special ammunition expended — all +1 sling bullets used, both special effect bullets lost. Sleep darts mostly used. Turnip has Protection from Evil still available. Wedge has Light still available. Healing potions used during the session. Each character has a healing touch ability (1 HP, once per day) — used during the session.

**Still at large:** Derek Desleigh and the surviving goblin, somewhere in the temple above. Misha's whereabouts unknown.

## Player Dynamics and Tone

**Les (Wedge):** The strategic driver. Frames key questions, pushes decisions, handles NPC diplomacy. Plays like a competent NCO. Self-aware enough to question his own reads.

**Stephen (Turnip):** The principled nuisance. Plays a high-INT, low-WIS hobbit with total sincerity. Creates productive chaos through earnest hobbit behavior. Asks the best questions at the worst moments. Demands apologies from constables. Mentions break-ins to the people whose houses he broke into.

**Todd (Boffo):** The quiet professional. Speaks least, lands hardest. Correctly reads situations while others build theories. Puts on half-moon reading glasses to study notes he can't read. Delivers one-line observations that reframe the conversation.

**The dynamic:** Les drives, Stephen creates chaos, Todd quietly steers. The comedy is organic and character-driven. The group treats the campaign as genuine escapism and creative outlet — the between-session emails with winter stories, ballads, and character vignettes are as much a part of the game as the sessions themselves.
