## A Hissing in the Rubble

The [Toad Stompers](/hobbity/world/pcs/) stood in a muddy tunnel, listening to a hissing somewhere ahead. The chamber it came from had fallen in on itself—the fire upstairs at the [Golden Grain](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane) had collapsed half the roof and most of the supports. Out of a corner of the rubble, a snake came worming up to take a bite at [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) and missed. [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) brought the cobra-headed mace down on its head with the easy thoroughness of a hobbit pulping a beet. The snake collapsed, dead.

"Don't overthink it," [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) said, satisfied. His luck had held; that bite carried the sort of venom that ends a hobbit, and he had not given it the chance. The party searched the room and found only mud and broken timbers. Whatever had been in here was buried under tons of dirt. They turned back to take a different passage.

## Tight Quarters

The hand-dug tunnels were tight in a way that made even hobbits crab-walk. [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), with his oversized pack of preparations, got himself wedged at one of the narrowest pinches.

"Hey, hey, hey, Wedge," [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) said. "I'm wedged in here."

[Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) pushed from behind, [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) pulled from the front, and Turnip popped through with a small ceremonial groan.

"These tunnels aren't too tight for a snake," [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) observed.

Somewhere off down a side passage, Turnip caught a scratching sound, too faint for the others to hear. He couldn't place it. They pressed on west, the lantern bobbing on [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/)'s belt, [Whiskers](/hobbity/world/npcs/#whiskers) flat on his head and not moving at all.

In the next slow stretch of tunnel, [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) noticed she had not moved in some time. Wedge reached up and touched her side. Warm. Breathing. Sound asleep. Wedge's eye went to his belt and there it was: the little ceramic jar from [Bertram](/hobbity/world/npcs/#bertram)'s kitchen, the lid not quite seated, a smear of musty paste on the rim.

"She'll sleep it off," said [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/), tucking the jar deeper into his pack and seating the lid this time. The weasel snored, very faintly, with the dignified satisfaction of a creature who had chosen her own fate.

## The Constrictor Above

They came into a wider chamber and [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) caught the glint of lantern-light off something coiled around a ceiling support. A constrictor, twenty feet of it, wrapped around the beam like a rope. It had the patience of an animal that has eaten this way before.

It did not get the drop on them. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) put an arrow up into its underside; the snake unspooled and came down hard beside Boffo, missing its strike against the chainmail. Turnip's sling-stone banged off the wall behind. Boffo swung high with the mace and missed, the ceiling cramping his arc. The snake recoiled to gather itself for another lunge.

[Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) drew his short sword and danced in behind the thing, hacking. Boffo brought the mace down on the tail. Somehow between the two of them the great length of the thing went still on the floor.

"That short sword's not magical," [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) said reasonably, eyeing the blade.

"Don't think you could convince me of that," said [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/), who had just used it to help kill a twenty-foot constrictor and was therefore prepared to defend its honour against all comers. He gave it a small swishing through the air, and admired it.

## A Door Made of Dirt

While the others were debating snakeskin belts and snakeskin suspenders, [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) noticed something about the alcove behind him. The wall there was wrong. Not wrong in the way muddy tunnels are wrong, but wrong in the way that something honest is made to look like something else: too rectangular, too neatly placed, the dirt suspiciously even.

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) chipped at it with the tip of his dagger. Clumps of mud came away. Under the mud was canvas, and under the canvas was wood—a pair of slap-together double doors that someone had cunningly packed over with dirt and paint to vanish them into the tunnel wall. Boffo levered them with the edge of his shovel and they came apart with the snap of a thing made in a hurry.

Inside: a closet, hardly three feet tall. Three chests stacked at the back.

"One for each of us," said [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), pleased.

## What Came From The Dark

What came out of the dark behind them was not a snake.

It was a troglodyte, more or less, in the way that a corpse is a person. Grey, claw-handed, hungry in a way nothing alive is hungry. A ghoul. It came scrambling out of the passage at [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/)'s back so suddenly that Turnip's first thought was to dig out his caltrops, which was no good against undead and no good even against the living because [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) had already stepped between him and it. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/)'s arrow went wide. Boffo's swing went wide too, distracted by the thing leaping up his chest. The ghoul raked him with one claw, and its teeth found his shoulder, and a slow stone cold climbed up Boffo's arms and legs and stopped his breathing in his throat.

He couldn't move. He couldn't close his eyes. He could feel his own heart and not much else.

[Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) reached for what magic he had and let it loose; it took a chunk out of the ghoul. The ghoul still had Boffo, and what the ghoul wanted was to eat. It bent down and bit, and Boffo, frozen, felt every part of it. Then it began to drag him by the shoulder back into the dark of the tunnel.

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), boggled, lunged for Boffo's dropped mace and could not get hold of it. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) ran a step and put an arrow through the back of the ghoul's skull point-blank, and the arrow came out of its mouth almost into Boffo's shoulder. The ghoul dropped where it stood.

Out of the dark behind it, at the very edge of the lantern's reach, came a second one.

## A Light in the Eyes

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) put a sling-stone through it before it could act. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) snapped his fingers and a clean white light bloomed across the second ghoul's eyes—not a flash, but a steady, awful brightness fixed onto the creature's eyes. It hissed, clawed at its own face, and scrabbled back the way it had come, blind. Wedge dropped his lantern at Turnip's feet and went after it with the bow.

The ghoul could not see Wedge in the dark, but Wedge could see the ghoul perfectly—its skull glowed through the tunnels ahead of him like a will-o'-the-wisp on legs, the light he had stuck to it travelling wherever it went. It stumbled, sometimes flailing towards Wedge's footfalls, sometimes wheeling away. Wedge missed once, then put an arrow through it, then another, and on the third the glowing head fell still in the mud and did not lift again. He came back to find Turnip with both hands under [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/)'s hairy ankles, hauling him an inch at a time towards the lantern.

## The Boffo Abides

Boffo could move his eyes and that was all. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) knelt and rubbed at Boffo's shoulders and arms, working the muscles. Slowly, the stone in Boffo's joints softened and let go. He blinked. His mouth came back. He drew his first proper breath in what felt like an age.

"That stuff is bad news," he said.

He laid his own hand on his shoulder, where the bite had taken, and called up a cure he had only just learned to call. The wound closed, leaving the chainmail bloodied and the day's lesson learned.

"The Boffo abides," he said.

## Three Empty Chests

[Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) checked the chests himself. No traps. All three were unlocked. All three were empty.

Whoever had hidden these here had also been the one to clear them out. [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), who had been keeping his eyes on the mud, noticed at the doorway a fresh set of footprints—deep, hobbity, heavy with the press of someone carrying looted treasure—leading west out of the chamber.

"They've gotten the hell out of dodge," said [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/). "We got here a little bit late."

"It had to be Derek," said [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/).

## Explictica Underground

They followed the prints west and came to a room where the way ahead had been freshly sapped. The earth was new-fallen, [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) saw a strip of white under the dirt and dug at it with his shovel, expecting bone. The shovel went _clunk_, and then _crack_, and Boffo felt the thing break under the spade.

It was a statue. An enormous one. Carved out of a single great tusk—ivory, the kind a mammoth or one of the old giant beasts might have left in the earth—with other pieces of ivory arranged around it to give the rest of its shape. A snake. With a woman's head. Coils set on a stone dais, and four arms held out in benediction or warning.

"[Explictica Defilus](/hobbity/world/npcs/#explictica-defilus)," said [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/), looking at what his shovel had just punched through the forehead of.

Intact, the thing would have been worth a small fortune to the wrong sort of buyer. In pieces, it was worth nothing to anybody and a great deal of guilt to Boffo. The cult had hidden their idol on the way out and brought the roof down on top of it. They had not wanted the goddess found.

## Block and Tackle

There was no getting the statue intact—Boffo had seen to that already. But the rubble it was buried in was the same rubble blocking the only way forward, and [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) thought that pulling the statue out might pull the cave-in with it. [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), of course, had a block and tackle and a rope, and pitons, and the smug, vindicated air of a hobbit who had been carrying these things for months waiting for an excuse.

The first yank popped the head off and sent it clattering back. The second got a substantial piece of coil and dais, and with it came down a great wash of the rubble blocking the passage. By the time they had dug a hobbit-sized hole through what remained—shovelling dirt along the line of three—the way west was open.

They piled what they could over the dead ghoul as a small rough burial, on Wedge's suggestion, and ducked through.

## Miles in the Dark

The tunnel beyond was the same hand-dug, three-foot, rickety nonsense as the rest, but longer. Much longer. They walked it for hours, cramped and stooped, the lantern oil burning down, the air thinning. By the time the tunnel ended, the late-afternoon they had left in Orlane had become full night.

The far end opened into a half-collapsed basement: stone floor, stone walls, an old stone staircase going up. Air came down it that smelled of forest and damp leaves, and overhead, through the open stair-mouth, a few stars showed.

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) recognised the build at once. It was the kind of basement they had stood in once before, in the bottom level of the Fortfield Tower at Huddle Farm—stone work older than hobbits, dug out by someone who had come along a great deal later. Not a hobbit ruin. Something older.

## A Hobbit on a Chain

They went up the stairs in marching order, Boffo singing to himself under his breath that he was being very, very sneaky. The roof above the staircase was open to the sky. A ruined courtyard. Low stone walls in the moonlight. Trees beyond.

Set in the middle of the courtyard was an iron stake driven deep into the earth. A chain ran from the stake to the ankle of a hobbit standing alone in the grass, hands on his thighs, head bowed, breath coming hard and not quite even. The hobbit, even at a distance, was unmistakable to Wedge.

It was [Gammy Boffin](/hobbity/world/npcs/#gammy-boffin). Judge of the [Gomwick](/hobbity/world/places/#gomwick) pie-eating contest. Father of Daisy, the baker. A long way from home.

To the southwest, beyond a stretch of broken wall, torchlight. Voices in the troglodyte tongue. A troglodyte sat on the wall with his back to the courtyard, looking out the other way. Past him, half-shielded by the ruins, goblins. The Toad Stompers ducked behind their own wall and counted, and the count was bad.

"Too many of them for us," said [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/).

"Way too many," said [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/).

Then the torchlight, slowly, began to move off. The voices receded. The troglodyte on the wall stayed.

[Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) checked his pack and found his thieves' tools where he had not remembered putting them, which was a relief. He would go alone, low and quiet, across the grass to the stake; the other two would wait by the stair, weapons drawn. A crow's caw if anything went wrong.

Wedge crept forward, lockpicks in hand.

## Conclusion

The cult's road out of [Orlane](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane) ended in a ruined forest courtyard a ways northwest of town, with a [Gomwick](/hobbity/world/places/#gomwick) friend chained to a stake and a wandering patrol just out of sight. The cult of [Explictica Defilus](/hobbity/world/npcs/#explictica-defilus) had gone ahead of them in a hurry—but they had been on the way to taking a [Gomwick](/hobbity/world/places/#gomwick) hobbit with them, and now [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) was crossing the grass with his picks out and his nerve held, while [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) and [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) crouched in the stair-mouth with their bows ready and a small, awful list of new things they had learned about troglodytes that had stopped being alive.
