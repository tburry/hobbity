## Entering the Temple

The party entered the Temple of Merikka posing as pilgrims seeking healing, bringing [Zacharias](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#zacharias)'s bag of gold. [Misha](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#misha), the priestess, healed Boffo with divine magic—a novelty for the hobbits.

![Misha](../../assets/images/npcs/misha.webp) During the visit they spotted Derek Desleigh, a scarred hobbit previously seen at the Golden Grain Inn, involved with goblins inside the temple.

## The Temple Fight

Derek and a group of goblins confronted the party. Derek wanted them taken alive. Turnip bluffed, yelling at Derek that the poison will take full effect in two minutes: "Use them wisely." The fight moved into the shadows and darker corners of the worship hall, where the goblins' infravision gave them an edge.

Boffo was knocked unconscious during the fight. The goblins' nerve broke; the surviving goblin and Derek fled north through a door. Turnip lunged for Derek as he ran but couldn't get a grip. The party regrouped rather than pursuing into darkness with a man down.

## The Jade Statue

The party eyed the massive jade statue of Merikka speculatively—a solid piece of jade weighing easily over a thousand pounds. Hauling it back to the Slumbering Serpent for guaranteed level two became a running joke, the party's imagined retirement fund.

## Merikka

Boffo pondered that Zacharias should know about this goddess, reasoning that she is from "the coast" and that's where their adventuring party did their adventuring based on discussions with [Buford](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#buford-niss).

## The Offering Room

Moving through the temple, the party found a hallway lined with golden food items—a carrot, beans, wheat—exceptionally well-crafted golden replicas, rendered with hyper-realistic detail including imperfections and bits of dirt. Turnip grabbed the golden carrot. No one stopped him. The world fell away—he was underwater, somewhere beautiful, and Merikka swam toward him. She kissed him. Something tugged at his mind, but Turnip held, and then he was back in the hallway, soaking wet, clutching a golden carrot.

![A vision of Merikka reaching out](../../assets/images/merikka-vision.webp)

## The Dungeon Beneath

In Misha's quarters the party found her arms and armour—chainmail, a painted shield bearing the crest of Merikka, and a mace. Boffo put them on. The chainmail was tight in the wrong places and loose in others, but it was better than nothing.

![The shield of Merikka, taken from Misha's quarters](../../assets/images/shield-of-merikka.webp)

A secret passage lay under the bed, opened by a torch mechanism. The stonework below is clearly much older than the temple above.

Boffo put it together: "Everybody kept telling us they couldn't build hobbit-holes on the side of this hill and yet here we are. Because the new temple was built over top of an old temple." Turnip corrected: "An old ruin. We don't know what it was." But the implication was clear—this hill has been dug into before, extensively. This contrasts to [Huddle Farm](/hobbity/appendix/places/#huddle-farm) where [Tom Huddle](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#tom-huddle) was actively repurposing the stone in the Fortfield—the stone with the weird snake symbols—for his own hobbit hole.

The passage below is a mix of constructed corridors and natural cave. Some corridors have iron spikes protruding from the walls. New internal walls have been built within larger original spaces, creating a maze-like layout. "This is a nonsensical structure, gentlemen."

## The Giant Lizard

The party opened a door and an emaciated, dog-sized giant lizard came to investigate. Boffo, jumpy from days of underground horrors, whacked it on the head with his mace before it could do anything. Only then did the lizard become aggressive in self-defence, and having tasted Wedge's blood it hungered for more.

The lizard was quick and leathery, and the party's blows glanced off its hide more often than not. Turnip's enchanted sling stones finally found their mark—two clean shots that cracked through scale and bone when fortune briefly remembered the hobbits existed.

## Underground Exploration

In the mud the party found troglodyte prints, confirming the creatures were using these passages regularly.

## The Troglodyte Pie Deduction

Wedge wondered about the inn that served troglodyte pie—were they in on it, or did they just stumble across a dead troglodyte and decide to serve it? The party concluded the innkeepers probably bested an attacking troglodyte and served it rather than waste the meat, and don't want to talk about it because then they'd have to admit to serving troglodyte pie.

## Troglodyte Lair

A giant mushroom in the cavern let out a piercing shriek the moment the hobbits drew near—a living alarm that echoed off the wet stone and brought troglodytes boiling out of the darkness. The party fought multiple waves in the underground stream and cave area, bottlenecking them in narrow passages. Boffo drank a Potion of Growth and became giant-sized, filling the tunnels and swinging his mace with devastating reach. They found a locked chest containing 420 gold pieces (140 each) and a Brooch of Shielding—another piece of Merikkan equipment, a ward that could absorb a great deal of punishment before shattering. Boffo pinned it to his coat, though it would later pass to Turnip when the front line shifted.

![The Brooch of Shielding found in the troglodyte lair](../../assets/images/brooch-of-shielding.webp)

## Conclusion

The party infiltrated the Temple of Merikka, fought off [Derek Desleigh](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#derek-desleigh)'s goblins, discovered ancient tunnels beneath, and cleared a troglodyte lair. They end deep underground with torches running low and Boffo still giant-sized.
