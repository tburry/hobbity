# The Summons

Between Sessions – 24 <dfn title="May">Thrimidge</dfn>, S.R. 1426

- **PCs:**
  - [Boffo Lunderbunk](/hobbity/appendix/pcs/boffo) - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
  - [Wedge Wedgerton](/hobbity/appendix/pcs/wedge) - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
  - [Turnip Bramblebrook](/hobbity/appendix/pcs/turnip) - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
- **Location:** [Thistledown](/hobbity/appendix/places/#thistledown), [The Right Way Inn](/hobbity/appendix/places/#the-right-way-inn), [Gomwick](/hobbity/appendix/places/#gomwick)

## Back to Gomwick

Early, but not too early as to have missed breakfast, on the morning of the 24th of May, the three hobbits met up at Turnip's front door, packed and ready to travel to Gomwick in answer to [Buford](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#buford-niss)'s letter. The meeting was reminiscent of the previous journey the three of them took, with the fine and pleasant mid-spring weather a comfort as they struck out onto the road.

Unlike the previous trip to Gomwick where they were forced to camp out in the woods, this time they were headed to The Right Way Inn, where they had planned to spend the night. The Right Way sat at the crossroads of the North Road and the Heart Road, and it was a well known and frequented establishment that had more in common with a small village than an Inn.

The day was pleasant and easy going, and they spent most of it chatting with fellow travellers and farmers they met along the way, even managing to hitch a ride on the back of a wagon a few times.

The Right Way was all that was promised, being lively and boisterous, and with plenty of good food and drink. The three of them paid for beds in the communal Long Room—a step up from sleeping on the dirty floors of the Common Room, but a definite step down from the more luxurious and expensive private rooms. No matter, as it was dry and warm and comfortable enough for the one night.

## Gomwick in Spring

The next morning, a little cooler with grey skies, they set off west along the Heart Road towards Gomwick, expecting to arrive just after mid-day.

Coming into the town of Gomwick, it felt strange seeing it without the throngs of crowds that characterized the festival last fall. The town seemed almost abandoned by comparison. It hit them that Gomwick, while larger than their own home village of Thistledown, was far smaller than they recalled from the festival days now that they saw it in its usual state.

The road into Gomwick led them past the Happy Hobbit Inn, and it was an act of great willpower that kept them moving past its doors as their stomachs ached with hunger. But they knew that good (and free!) food and drink awaited at the top of the hill at Buford's house. They pushed on, past the town proper, receiving a few "Good days!" and "How'd ya fare?"s along the way. More than a few hobbits recognized Boffo in his feathered yellow hat, and they were forced to stop and shake some hands as well.

Finally, they pushed past, and meandered their way up the switchbacks that led to Toppo Hill House, arriving with sore legs, parched throats, and expectant bellies.

## Toppo Hill House

They knocked and rang the bell, and it wasn't long before [Dalton Dallydown](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#dalton-dallydown) opened the door and received them. They remembered Dalton as Buford's butler and seeming right-hand in all manner of his life. Though he was excellent at not being seen as he did his duty, it was him that greeted them today.

Dalton looked overtired and concerned, as he politely greeted them and took their cloaks and other things and hung them neatly by the door. He deferred politely and refused to answer any questions or become engaged in conversation.

During this brief moment as they stood in the entryway of Toppo Hill, they noticed that much had changed in the intervening months. While the house had seemed exuberant, today it was subdued. In the fall, every candle had seemed to be lit, every hearth with a warm fire, and every room occupied with some semblance of light or use. Today, the house seemed cold and empty. In fact, many of Buford's prized possessions—which they had noticed bore a distinct nautical and exotic theme—were gone.

Dalton led them to the same parlour where they had talked with Buford in the aftermath of the Scarecrow business and from whence they set off to find [the Hermit](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#the-hermit). The room looked as if no one had been in here in months, and Dalton took pains to open curtains to bring in some light.

Once he saw them comfortable, he left for a few moments and returned with a tray of refreshments—much more meager than what they remembered—and excused himself again to inform them that his master would be along shortly.

As he did so, a figure entered the parlour from the other side.

## A Changed Hobbit

Buford Niss was much changed. The hobbit they saw before them had lost a significant amount of weight, looking almost gaunt. He wore a dressing gown over his clothes, and over that he had a wool blanket draped over his shoulders. He walked with difficulty, and with the assistance of a cane.

However, as he entered the room, they could see the spark of life still in his eyes, and he had genuine joy at seeing the three of them here in his home once more. He greeted each of them warmly, asking about their families and their winter, and if they had much trouble on the road. He looked at the poor offerings of food that Dalton had provided, and insisted that better sustenance be served. Dalton shot him a concerned glance, but Buford was undeterred and demanded that proper food should be served to his guests, and that Dalton should run down to the Happy and bring up whatever stew and dishes they had made that day.

Once the pleasantries had taken place, Buford practically sank down into an arm chair and needed a moment to recover.

Then he turned to the three of them and, with some difficulty speaking, but evident excitement in his voice, got to the crux of why they were here:

"Thank you, my friends, for agreeing to come. It means a great deal to me that you are here and are willing to help me.

First, I should tell you that over the winter I did receive word from Our Friend in the Woods—just as he promised he would—and it has only obfuscated the Scarecrow matter further.

I won't bore you with the cryptic details, but the message he sent was simply the following: 'The crows are scared because the serpent hungers.'

Well, I did not know what to make of it, if I must be honest! And I have spent much of the winter pondering on it and trying to figure it out.

Recently, though while I was thinking on it, I remembered about my little statue. You see, many, many years ago during my travels, I acquired this beautiful piece of art made out of solid jade. It was an intricately carved coiled snake, with its head reared up very majestically. You could see each individual scale. It was lovely! And that wasn't all. You see its head was likewise delicately made with great artistry out of a solid piece of gold, with two bright rubies for eyes. But the greatest surprise was that you could remove the golden head, and beneath it was the face of an extraordinarily beautiful woman. It truly was an amazing piece of art! I was told it was very, very ancient and also very, very expensive!"

At this Buford gave a hearty laugh, which sent him into a coughing fit that took a while to subside.

"Anyway, now the problem is that it has gone missing. I've searched up and down and every which way in this house, and it is nowhere to be found.

I have come to believe that the Scarecrow incident, and the message from the old Hermit, and now the missing statue are all related somehow.

But, odd as that may all be, this is not actually why I have summoned you here today. No, that is a labyrinth whose entrance we have not yet found. There are no real leads that we can follow yet.

But, I do require your help, but for an altogether different task. As you can see, I am in no state to leave my home."

Almost to emphasize the point, Buford began another hacking coughing fit. He wiped his mouth with a handkerchief, and Wedge noticed that the little pocket square was speckled with blood.

## The Mission

"Here," he said as he took a sip of brandy to soothe his throat, "Read this aloud, please." And he handed a piece of paper to Turnip.

> Much has happened in my life since we fought side by side in the troll war. I hope, sometime, to have a chance to talk with you about the many pleasant things.
>
> My purpose now, sadly, is to ask—nay, beg—my courageous comrades to aid my people in a time of dire need. I cannot even describe to you the danger that threatens Orlane, for I know not its true nature. I do know that, unless it can somehow be stopped, this evil will consume my little village and its families. We will vanish without trace into the dust of history.
>
> A sinister force is at work here, and it is made all the more frightening by the fact that its true nature is concealed in a web of fear and suspicion. I plead with you come to Orlane, lend your skills to revealing this menace, that it may finally be destroyed!
>
> Your comrade,\
> _Zacharias Ormond_

Once Turnip had finished reading, Buford spoke:

"Something must be truly wrong if [Zacharias](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#zacharias) is reaching out to me like this. We haven't spoken in quite a long time, and didn't part in the best of terms. This letter is very unexpected. And concerning.

Especially now that I think back on the Festival and recall how odd it was that so few visitors from [Orlane](/hobbity/appendix/places/#orlane) came to it. And those that did come, seemed to be rather grumpy and reserved; not at all what I recall from such a prosperous and gentle little village like Orlane.

What I am asking of you is to travel to Orlane and seek out my friend Zacharias. Find out exactly what has him in such a bother.

You'll enjoy Orlane. There's a wonderful Inn there called the Golden Grain. Some of the finest beer and bread in all the Shires! Tell the owner [Bertram](/hobbity/appendix/npcs/#bertram) that Buford sent you, and I'm sure he'll treat you right. And he'd better give you a discount!

Come to think of it, there was another Inn in Orlane, called the Soaking Serpent…? Or the Sneaking Snake? I can't remember the name. Awful place. But we were just talking about snakes, weren't we? That's a funny thing.

In any case, Orlane is about 2 or 3 days travel southwest-ish of here. You'll have to go down some backroads, and not the Heart Road, but it's pleasant, hilly, farm country with some scattered woods.

Will you do this for me?"

## Outfitting

The three hobbits accepted. Buford offered to reward them handsomely upon their return.

"In anticipation of your arrival, I've taken the liberty to prepare a few supplies for your travels. I hope you do not mind," he said.

Buford provided each of them with a set of brand new leather armour, a dagger, a sling with twenty stone bullets, a good quality leather backpack, and a wool travel cloak. He also gave them a purse of 30 gold coins for travel and outfitting expenses.

Then the individual gifts:

For Turnip, he produced the beautiful blowgun he had lent them previously, along with a box of ten darts. "I remember you eyeing this the last time you were here, and you were decent enough to bring it back to me intact. I'd like you to have it as a gift from me. I have a feeling I won't be needing it again, and such things should be in useful hands, not sitting away rotting in a chest. The darts are specially coated with an oil that will cause your target to fall asleep, so be careful not to prick your finger on them."

For Boffo: "My boy, you're a true champion! I have this elixir to help you when you need it. Drink it down when you feel you're in a pinch and you'll grow twice your size! Just be careful when you use it!"

For Wedge: "I know you've already got a good axe from Lotho, so I didn't think you'd need another weapon. Instead I got you a quick escape, should you need it. Drink this down and you will vanish from sight long enough to get away."

Sufficient food and drink for three days' travel was loaded up, and the three hobbits set about preparing for the road to Orlane.
