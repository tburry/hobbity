# The Eye Beneath the Hill

Session Recap – 18 <dfn title="December">Foreyule</dfn>, S.R. 1425

- **PCs:**
  - Boffo Lunderbunk - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
  - Wedge Wedgerton - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
  - Turnip Bramblebrook - Level 1 Hobbit Adventurer
- **Location:** Fortfield Tower, Huddle Farm

## Into the Tower

The old wizard's tower on the Huddle Farm property had stood quiet long enough. The three hobbits descended into its basement levels, starting with living quarters on the first floor down. Buckets of green paint and a scrawled warning about "the Eye" answered one question—whoever had been painting the Huddle cows worked from here. Small hobbit footprints in the dust confirmed it wasn't some outsider's prank.

## Things That Bite and Things That Float

A crab spider in the second-level workshop was dispatched without ceremony. The library one floor below proved worse. A crystal hung suspended in the air—the wizard's old security measure—and it opened fire the moment they entered. The fight dragged on, the crystal drifting out of reach and taking shots while the party scrambled for angles. Boffo finally landed the strike that shattered it. In the summoning room beneath that waited "the Eye"—a floating sphere that rotated through elemental discharges: lightning that bounced off walls, fire that caught clothing, poison that sapped strength, cold that locked joints in place. Wedge, Turnip, and Boffo read its pattern quickly and slipped past without trying to destroy it.

## The Well and What It Held

The fifth and lowest level opened into an unfinished room with a natural tunnel running to caverns beneath the farm's well. There they found Nailer, the Huddle family dog, half-dissolved in green slime. The pieces fit: something had poisoned the toads above the well, Nailer had gone after a tainted toad, sickened, and tumbled in. Behind a locked door—Turnip's nimble fingers made short work of it—sat a treasure room. Two empty chests, several broken ones, and a single heavy locked chest they chose to leave sealed. Prudence or paranoia, hard to say. The paint evidence alone was worth the trip.

## Conclusion

Five levels down and back up again, the party emerged with proof that the cow-painting culprit had used the tower as a base, and with more questions about the wizard who built the place. Nailer's fate was grim but explained, and one locked chest still waits below.

### Enemies Defeated

- 1 crab spider
- 1 floating crystal
- 1 floating eye (bypassed)

### Treasure

None taken (locked chest left unopened).
