## A Borrowed Dress

The Toad Stompers woke in the afternoon, sore and slightly richer for it.

[Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) tracked down [Belba](/hobbity/world/npcs/#belba) and asked, with as much delicacy as a hobbit can muster, whether she could spare a dress. [Belba](/hobbity/world/npcs/#belba) raised one eyebrow, looked Boffo up and down, and produced a serviceable working dress of her own. "I don't think it's going to fit," she said. Boffo held it up against himself, beamed, and said it would fit just perfect.

## A Visit to Ramne

[Whiskers the weasel](/hobbity/world/npcs/#whiskers) was sitting on the roof of the half-buried cottage, sunning herself, when the Toad Stompers came up the elm-shaded path. Inside, [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) had a stew on the hearth that smelled odd but not bad, and [Cirilli Finla](/hobbity/world/npcs/#cirilli-finla) was asleep on a cot by the wall, still in the rags she'd been wearing for a week in her cage. The old druid lifted [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/)'s chin, peered into [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/)'s eyes, slapped [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) on the back, and pronounced them all considerably less awful than the night before.

He'd been in the woods, he said, looking for the trail Cirilli had described—abducted hobbits marched blindfolded to a wilderness clearing where [Abramo](/hobbity/world/npcs/#abramo) had done his charming work. He'd never found it. The woods kept turning him around, spitting him back out where he hadn't meant to go. He'd always thought it was just the trees being trees, wanting to be left alone, the way trees do.

Boffo asked, quietly, whether he'd ever felt the trees were _looking_ at him. [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) paused, and waved at one out his window. Boffo pressed: not friendly looking. Sinister. Like in the Old Wood on the road to Gomwick, where the three of them had nearly been swallowed up. [The Hermit](/hobbity/world/npcs/#the-hermit) had said it was old magic come back. [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) listened, and his face did something complicated.

He produced a tatty map and laid it on the table. To the northwest—past Orlane, past the Dimwood—there was a swamp called the Rushmoors. Reptilian things liked swamps. The marching distance Cirilli had described would put their wilderness chapel somewhere in those woods, and a base of operations somewhere in that swamp. The trouble was getting there: every time he tried, the Dimwood politely declined.

He couldn't lead them, but he could send something for the road. He took three ceramic bottles down from a shelf and ladled stew into each from his pot, sealed them with cork, and pressed one into each hobbit's hands. A homeopathic alchemical brew of his own devising, he said—a sip will do for a small wound, the whole bottle for a largish one, but it does go off, so don't dawdle. [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) tipped his straight back and drained it before [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) had finished explaining. [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) sighed, refilled it, and ladled out the stew again.

"Are you sure this heals?" [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) asked, swallowing. "Medicine's not usually this good."

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) asked if [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) himself was coming. [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) looked sadly at Cirilli on her cot, then at his own ancient legs, and said no—he'd slow them worse than Turnip, and the girl needed someone to look after her. But he could lend them his weasel. He spoke a few clipped words in a language none of the hobbits recognised—Druidic, he allowed, in answer to [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/)'s prying—and Whiskers came scurrying through the window, climbed Wedge's leg, up his back, and settled on his head with the air of a creature who had done this many times before. She was, [Ramne](/hobbity/world/npcs/#ramne) noted approvingly, very good at killing snakes.

[Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) laid Belba's dress folded at the foot of [Cirilli](/hobbity/world/npcs/#cirilli-finla)'s cot, nodded once, and the Toad Stompers were off.

## A Village Half on Fire

Outside, [Constable Grover](/hobbity/world/npcs/#constable-grover) was haggard. He hadn't slept; he'd spent the night wrangling a mob and the morning at the temple cataloguing the prisoners the hobbits had left him. A small crowd had gathered at the constabulary, bristling families demanding the release of relatives now locked in the disused warehouse a few doors down. [Haskelly](/hobbity/world/npcs/#haskelly) the money-changer was the loudest of them, demanding his son [Wil](/hobbity/world/npcs/#wil)—gone these many months—be let out, though Wil was not actually in the warehouse and never had been. [Grover](/hobbity/world/npcs/#constable-grover) caught the Toad Stompers' eye over the heads of the angry hobbits and managed a grim, exhausted nod. The general store across the square was shut tight; [Wilbur Oldbuck](/hobbity/world/npcs/#wilbur-oldbuck) was nowhere visible.

The [Golden Grain Inn](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane) was no longer on fire, but it was still smoking gently around the edges. The structure stood, more or less, with the kitchen end caved in and the western wall mostly down. Two soot-blackened hobbits were sifting through the wreckage with shovels.

## Visiting Opal

[Killian Gade](/hobbity/world/npcs/#killian-gade)'s stable was a peeling, manure-spotted shambles of a building, but [Opal](/hobbity/world/npcs/#opal), who is a good girl, was glossy, well-fed, and entirely beloved by the three small Gade children, who tumbled out of the house barefoot and covered in dirt to inform the Toad Stompers, in great detail, of every meal Opal had eaten and every kindness rendered. Their parents [Killian](/hobbity/world/npcs/#killian-gade) and [Iris](/hobbity/world/npcs/#iris-gade) seemed honest folk who simply preferred their gold went to feed than to paint.

While [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) brushed Opal down, [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) drew [Killian](/hobbity/world/npcs/#killian-gade) aside and asked the obvious question: who, in this town, was missing? [Killian](/hobbity/world/npcs/#killian-gade) and Iris exchanged the worried look of people who had asked themselves the same thing many times. [Maddie Snowvale](/hobbity/world/npcs/#maddie-snowvale)'s whole household, gone for some time now and only Maddie come back, and not himself when he did. [Kaylin](/hobbity/world/npcs/#kaylin-snowvale), who supposedly went to visit her grandmother on the coast and never returned. [Quinn Finla](/hobbity/world/npcs/#quinn-finla) and his wife Elena had gone away for a few days and come back without their two children—gone off to seek their fortunes, or so the story went. And Haskelly's boy Wil, missing the longest, who Haskelly was even now trying to free from a warehouse he wasn't in.

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/), meanwhile, had picked up a shovel and was attempting to bury his solid-gold carrot in [Opal](/hobbity/world/npcs/#opal)'s manure pile. He proved unequal to the task. The youngest Gade child wandered up, squinted, and said "that's not how you do it," and took the shovel from him. Two minutes later the middle child shoved his sibling aside, declared _that_ wasn't how you did it either, and took the shovel for himself. [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) stood there, dignity exhausted, watched his hole vanish into pristine stable hygiene, and quietly walked off to find a rock by the lake to push his carrot under instead.

[Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/), trusting hobbit, spied Opal's saddlebags piled in the corner of the barn. He lifted the lid of one, dropped his giant gold potato inside, draped a saddle-blanket over it, and stepped back to inspect his work. The corner of the potato gleamed unmistakably out from under the blanket. [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) squinted at it, gave it a satisfied nod, and walked away.

## Sifting the Golden Grain

The two soot-blackened hobbits at the rubble straightened up gratefully when the Toad Stompers approached. [Atticus](/hobbity/world/npcs/#atticus) introduced himself and his lad [Rolo](/hobbity/world/npcs/#rolo), handed over the shovels with great relief, and headed off to a long-overdue afternoon meal. The Toad Stompers were now alone in the burnt inn.

In the common room, the air still tasted of smoke. Most things were ruined; one thing was not. On a sideboard, untouched, sat an unopened bottle of Orlane Special—the same wine [Bertram](/hobbity/world/npcs/#bertram) had pressed into their hands the first day they'd walked into [Orlane](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane), the same wine they'd been served at the [Slumbering Serpent](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane), and the same wine [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) had developed a strong suspicion about ever since he'd noticed how very deeply everyone in this town slept. He pocketed the bottle.

They picked their way back to the private rooms. The first held only crockery. The second held [Bertram](/hobbity/world/npcs/#bertram).

He was on his bed, in his clothes, eyes shut. From the doorway he looked like he'd died of smoke. [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) crept closer, and saw the dark line across his throat where someone had drawn a blade ear to ear. The fire had been a cover. [Bertram](/hobbity/world/npcs/#bertram) had been murdered before the inn was lit.

## What Bertram Brewed

The kitchen was a wreck of smashed crockery, spilled flour, scattered beans, and dried meat trampled into the floor. Among the ruin, [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) found a small ceramic jar with a cork stopper, intact, full of an odd-smelling paste. He uncorked it, dabbed a careful fingertip on his tongue, and a slow, fuzzy heaviness began to steal up behind his eyes.

He set his teeth and powered through it. He'd had a taste of it before, in a glass of complimentary wine on his first day in [Orlane](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane), when [Bertram](/hobbity/world/npcs/#bertram) had stood smiling behind the bar; he'd been the only one of them, that afternoon, to shake it off. He recognised the feeling now. He corked the jar and slipped it into his pocket as evidence.

## Down the Hatch

The kitchen stair was buried, but a brewer's slanted door on the west side of the inn opened straight onto the cellar steps. [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) lit his lantern, and [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) led the way down.

Past a cool keg-room and into the proper cellar—tables stacked, cobwebs heavy, air damp. In the south wall stood a section of stone that didn't line up: a secret door, standing wide open. The room beyond had a carpet, a table, and an empty bookcase. Rats scattered through a second door, and behind them came what the cellar didn't have. Heat. The close, wet heat of a swamp at noon, stewed up out of nothing in the middle of an Orlane spring.

The smell came with it. Faint, oily, musty. Troglodyte.

## The Sweltering Tunnel

The south door was new—fresh wood, a working hinge—and beyond it the floor dropped into mud. A passage continued, its ceiling low even by hobbit standards, perhaps three and a half feet high. It had been hastily dug; timbers shoring it up every few feet were rough and crooked. A trickle of water ran along the bottom. [Tom Huddle](/hobbity/world/npcs/#tom-huddle) would have wept to see this carpentry.

In the mud were tracks. Bare hobbit feet. Long-clawed troglodyte feet. A great many of both, going both ways, churned up by heavy traffic. The rats, the heat, the smell—this was the road out of Orlane the cult had been using all along.

The passage was narrow enough that one hobbit would block another from passing. [Boffo](/hobbity/world/pcs/boffo/) ducked his head and went first, mace held low, shield up; [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/) followed with the lantern; [Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) brought up the rear. Whiskers rode on Wedge's head. Side passages opened off to the south. The heat thickened the further they went, until breathing was like sucking in stew, and the smell of troglodyte came along with it.

## A Hissing in the Dark

The passage opened into a wider chamber. The hobbits stopped. Somewhere ahead, somewhere to the west, something was telegraphing its displeasure at being disturbed—a long, low, scraping hiss with the unmistakable rasp of an angry animal building up to violence.

[Turnip](/hobbity/world/pcs/turnip/) recognised it first. "It's a snake," he whispered, voice tight. "It's an angry snake."

The lantern showed only mud and rough wall and the suggestion, somewhere ahead, of rubble and shadow.

Whiskers, on [Wedge](/hobbity/world/pcs/wedge/)'s head, went very still.

## Conclusion

A day after breaking the cult of [Explictica Defilus](/hobbity/world/npcs/#explictica-defilus) beneath the Temple of [Merikka](/hobbity/world/npcs/#merikka), the Toad Stompers spent the afternoon picking up the pieces—a dress for a freed prisoner, a borrowed weasel from an old druid, a beloved pony brushed and boarded, gold hidden from strangers, and themselves—before laying eyes on the only proper way out of Orlane the cult had built: a hot, low, hand-dug troglodyte road snaking south from the cellar of the [Golden Grain](/hobbity/world/places/#orlane) toward whatever awaits them in the Rushmoors. They have three bottles of suspicious stew, the trail at last, and something hissing in the dark just ahead.
