Wedge Wedgerton is the self-appointed leader of the Toad Stompers. Third-generation nickname—his great-grandfather's axe was called "Thin Edge of the Wedge"—and he's taken that literally as his role in the party. He talks the most and the fastest, drives every decision, and is affectionately impatient with his companions for not keeping up. Slightly too confident in his own read of every situation, but honest when he's wrong. He carries secrets the others don't know about and questions that won't leave him alone.

## Alignment – Chaotic

Wedge is Chaotic not as disorder but as appetite. He spent the winter practicing petty thefts he always undid, stealing for the thrill and returning the goods once he'd savoured the clandestine victory—because the act mattered more than the take. His own philosophy says it plainly: adventure means putting aside your manners and doing the sticky job others won't, and he has "a desire for further impertinence" that he fully intends to feed. He distrusts institutions on instinct—constables, temples, patrons who send hobbits on errands without telling them the whole truth. He broke into Zacharias's house without a second thought and called it polite. He drew himself up on hay bales and cast Light at a snake priest with a flourish, and when it bounced off he just swore and kept fighting, because Wedge would rather act boldly and be wrong than deliberate and be safe. Rules bore him, caution irritates him, and consensus is something that happens after he's already ten feet ahead. None of this makes him selfish—he coined "the Toad Stompers," drags his companions forward by sheer force of will, and genuinely believes every decision he makes is for their benefit. He's just never once considered asking them first.

## Personality

The self-appointed leader, whether anyone asked for one or not. Wedge talks the most and the fastest. He's affectionately impatient with his companions, always ten feet ahead and annoyed about it. His confidence bumps into reality regularly—sneaking with a lit lantern, casting spells that bounce off the enemy—but he pushes forward anyway. Handles encounters through sheer persistence and volume; folk tend to do what he says just to get a moment's peace. In a crisis he leads with spirit, not caution. Names things, claims things, drives decisions. He coined "the Toad Stompers" and will not let anyone forget it.
